Community Projects

We currently have one funded project and several potential projects that are out for consultation. We will be starting a survey to get some feedback on these projects. The survey will be sent by post and available online in early April. However, please feel free to contact us at any time with any feedback relating to any or all of these projects. We also welcome anyone to submit an idea for a project they would like to see realised - contact@westdidscommunity.co.uk.

Over 50s Coffee Afternoon - Funded

We have secured funding for a coffee afternoon for over 50s in Christ church hall. It will take place on alternate Tuesday’s from 1-4pm. This was the only slot available. We aim to start the sessions in mid June and would like to get ideas from the community about what they would like to do with these sessions. We will provide refreshments and games, but we also have funds to provide additional activities, such as craft sessions and workshops. We do not intend to organise such activities without sufficient input from those who wish to attend the sessions.

We are also looking for any volunteers who would be happy to help us run these sessions.

Send us feedback about this project

Edging for the young trees in Mersey Bank Park - Out for Consultation

The young trees in Mersey Bank Park are not flourishing as much as they could be. This is partly because there are no edges around the trees, so the grass grows right up to the trunk and competes with the trees for nutrients. We would like to install some edging for the young trees to help them grow. We have discussed this idea with the park ranger and are still awaiting a quote for the work. There are 50 trees in the park that would benefit from having edging installed.

Costs: Currently unknown

West Didsbury Estate Historical Archive - Out for Consultation

An online archive dedicated to the history of the West Didsbury esatate. There are many people who have lived on the estate for many years, some for their whole lives. There is so much history that our residents can share and we would like to record that history and make it available to all residents through an online archive. The archive can be used to store photos, videos and interviews with residents, either recorded or in writing.

Costs: Free.

Send us feedback about this project

Picnic in the park - Out for Consultation

A community picnic held on the large field in the late summer. The idea is simply to organise a date when people all come to the playing field with a picnic and enjoy some food while hopefully getting to know more people on the estate.

Costs: Free.

Send us feedback about this project

West Didsbury Estate Art Competition - Out for Consultation

An art competition for children and adults, including entries of short stories, poems, drawings/paintings and photos. There would be prizes for the winner and runner up in each category. All winning artwork will be posted on the community website, and we will print a book containing as many pieces as we can print in an 80 page book. The physical book and ebook would be available from lulu.com for residents to buy at an expected cost of £5 and £1 respectively. All profits would be spent by the group on other community projects.

Costs: Approximately £200 for prizes, however we would also look for some sponsorship.

Send us feedback about this project

Men’s Walking Club - Out for Consultation

A walking club for men on the estate, with organised monthly walks in the local area. The club is intended to help bring together men with a shared interest in walking and to take advantage of the extensive green spaces in the area.

Costs: Free.

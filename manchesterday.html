What is expected of me?

The idea is to get a group of people together, work with a local artist to decide on an idea we would like to bring to life, and with their help, design basic costumes and construct a large structure to reflect that idea. Then on the day, we get together in Deansgate to walk with our structure along the parade route.

Involvement can vary according to what you able to do. Ultimately, we need you to turn up on the day and walk in the parade, but there are several opportunities to get involved in the creative process. You do not need to be able to come to all the sessions, but it would be best if you could come to a couple.

  • Mid-April - Brainstorming session, to come up with ideas you would like to portray. We work with a local artist to think about what the concept we would like to portra,y based on this years theme, and the creative ways we can reflect it.
  • End April - Finalise the idea
  • May-early June - Making sessions. We work with a local artist to bring the idea to life. The number of making sessions depends on the complexity of the design, but for not all volunteers will be required for all sessions.
  • Weekend 2nd June - Workshop weekend. Either a morning or an aftenroon session at a warehouse near the site in Deansgate to put everything together ready for the day.
  • Weekend 9th June - Briefing before the big day.

What is this years theme?

The theme this year is Word on the street. This is meant to be loosely interpreted and can be used simply to reflect what Manchester, and your area in particular, means to you. The idea is to think of something positive you would like to say and reflect this creatively using costume and large constructions.

What is the day like?

For those involved in the parade, the schedule of the day looks something like this:

  1. Arrive at Liverpool road. We will be given a start time, and our starting position will be marked on the road.
  2. Pick up small items/costumes from the warehouse.
  3. Large items moved into place by a professional team.
  4. Parade officially starts at 1 pm. However, depending on our start position we may not start moving until up to half an hour later.
  5. Walk the parade route. The route is 1.25 miles and should take about 45 minutes to walk. There will be no shade or cover during the walk and it will go ahead whatever the weather.
  6. The parade finishes in Exchange Square at approximately 2:30, though again the exact finish time will depend on our position in the parade.

The parade route is available in the official programme

How do I get involved?

To register to take part, simply send us an email (contact@westdidscommunity.co.uk) or give us a call (07396-177378). We need to confirm numbers for those who will attend on the day by mid-April. Bear in mind that unfortunately this year Manchester day coincides with Father's day.

If you can't commit to geting involved with the group, come along and watch the parade anyway. There are lots of events on through the day to entertain everyone.
